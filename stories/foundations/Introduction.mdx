  {/* ── What are design tokens ── */}
  <div>
    <span className="fnd-section-label">The Basics</span>
    <h2 className="fnd-section-title">What is a design token?</h2>
    <div className="fnd-body">A design token is a named decision. Not <code>#e8195a</code> — but <strong>interactive-primary-bg</strong>. Not <code>16px</code> — but <strong>space-4</strong>. Tokens lift raw values out of stylesheets and give them meaning, context, and a home they can be referenced from anywhere — in Figma, in code, in documentation.</div>
    <div className="fnd-body">The moment a product designer changes a brand color in Figma, that change shouldn't require hunting through a dozen component files. With tokens, it propagates everywhere automatically — every button, badge, and border that references the token updates in one pass.</div>

    <div className="fnd-callout">
      <span className="fnd-callout-text"><em>Tokens are not styles.</em> They are the contract between design and engineering — a shared language that makes handoff a non-event and makes systematic change feel inevitable rather than painful.</span>
    </div>
  </div>

  {/* ── Why tokens exist ── */}
  <div>
    <span className="fnd-section-label">The Why</span>
    <h2 className="fnd-section-title">Built for scale. Built to last.</h2>
    <div className="fnd-body">AvilaDS was built in the context of fintech and iGaming products — environments where brand trust is everything, theming requirements are complex, and visual consistency directly affects user confidence. Tokens aren't an academic exercise here. They are how we ship <strong>dark mode without duplication</strong>, support <strong>white-label theming</strong> without branching components, and maintain <strong>accessibility standards</strong> across every surface.</div>
    <div className="fnd-body">AvilaDS uses a three-tier token architecture: primitives hold raw values, semantic tokens assign meaning to those values, and component tokens bind semantics to specific UI contexts. Each tier exists so that decisions at the right level of abstraction stay there — and don't leak downward.</div>

    <div className="fnd-tier-grid">
